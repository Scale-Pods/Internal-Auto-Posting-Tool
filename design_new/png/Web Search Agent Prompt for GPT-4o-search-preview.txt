RESEARCH METHODOLOGY
-----------------------------------

PHASE 1: PRIMARY DIGITAL ASSET DISCOVERY
Search and locate the following for [COMPANY_NAME]:

1. Official Website
   - Homepage URL
   - Product/Service pages
   - Pricing pages
   - About Us / Team pages
   - Blog/Resources section
   - Case Studies / Success Stories
   - Careers page

2. Social Media Profiles
   - LinkedIn (company page)
   - Instagram (official handle)
   - Twitter/X (official handle)
   - Facebook (business page)
   - YouTube (channel)

3. Business Listings
   - Google Business Profile
   - Crunchbase
   - G2/Capterra (if SaaS)
   - Industry directories

-----------------------------------
PHASE 2: DEEP CONTENT ANALYSIS
-----------------------------------

For WEBSITE content, extract and analyze:

A. Product/Service Understanding
   - Core offerings (products/services)
   - Pricing model (freemium, subscription, one-time, enterprise)
   - Key features and capabilities
   - Technology stack (if visible)
   - Integrations supported

B. Brand Positioning & Messaging
   - Value proposition (headline, tagline)
   - Positioning statement (premium, affordable, enterprise, SMB, etc.)
   - Key differentiators (what makes them unique)
   - Brand personality traits (innovative, trustworthy, disruptive, etc.)

C. Tone & Voice Analysis
   - Writing style (formal, casual, technical, conversational)
   - Language complexity (simple, jargon-heavy, accessible)
   - Emotional tone (aspirational, practical, urgent, calm)
   - Consistency across pages

D. Target Audience (ICP) Indicators
   - Industry focus (if specified)
   - Company size mentions (enterprise, SMB, startups)
   - Geographic markets
   - Job roles/personas mentioned
   - Pain points addressed

E. Content Inventory
   - Blog topics and themes
   - Content depth (surface-level vs detailed)
   - Funnel stage coverage:
     * TOFU (awareness): educational, thought leadership
     * MOFU (consideration): comparison, features, use cases
     * BOFU (decision): pricing, case studies, demos, ROI
   - Content formats (articles, videos, infographics, tools)
   - Publishing frequency (if dates visible)

F. Website Conversion Elements
   - Primary CTAs (sign up, demo, contact, free trial)
   - Lead magnets (ebooks, templates, tools)
   - Trust signals (testimonials, logos, certifications)
   - Navigation clarity
   - Mobile optimization indicators

-----------------------------------
PHASE 3: SOCIAL MEDIA INTELLIGENCE
-----------------------------------

For each active social platform, analyze:

A. LinkedIn Analysis
   - Follower count
   - Posting frequency (posts per week)
   - Content types (company updates, thought leadership, hiring, events)
   - Engagement patterns (likes, comments, shares)
   - Employee advocacy (are employees sharing?)
   - Thought leadership topics

B. Instagram Analysis
   - Follower count
   - Visual style (professional, lifestyle, UGC, product-focused)
   - Content mix (posts, reels, stories, carousels)
   - Brand aesthetics (colors, filters, consistency)
   - Bio and CTA
   - Engagement rate indicators

C. Twitter/X Analysis
   - Handle and follower count
   - Tweet frequency and timing
   - Content themes (support, announcements, engagement, thought leadership)
   - Tone (serious, playful, responsive)
   - Community interaction

-----------------------------------
PHASE 4: COMPETITOR INTELLIGENCE (if competitors provided)
-----------------------------------

For each competitor, extract:
1. Positioning differences
2. Messaging angle
3. Key features they emphasize
4. Content strategy differences
5. Social media maturity
6. Unique tactics or campaigns
7. Pricing positioning

-----------------------------------
PHASE 5: GAP & OPPORTUNITY IDENTIFICATION
-----------------------------------

Based on your research, identify:

1. Content Gaps
   - Missing funnel stage content
   - Underrepresented topics
   - Weak storytelling areas

2. Messaging Gaps
   - Unclear value proposition
   - Weak differentiation
   - Inconsistent tone

3. Channel Gaps
   - Underutilized platforms
   - Low engagement channels
   - Missing channel opportunities

4. Competitive Gaps
   - What competitors do better
   - Opportunities they're missing
   - White space in the market

5. Conversion Gaps
   - Weak CTAs
   - Missing trust elements
   - Friction points
